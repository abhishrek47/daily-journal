*(Retrospective — reconstructed April 14 2026)*

Two conversations. One moment that was more emotional than expected.

**Capgemini L&D Consultant**: a role within Claims Training, under the Design umbrella. Designing and facilitating learning solutions for Claims and FBS (Financial and Business Services). The facilitation component is the flag — post-COVID I moved deliberately away from facilitation and toward instructional design and systems work. That's not a gap in my skills, it's a deliberate direction change. Still: Capgemini is a good company. Decided to give it a go, get the conversation started. Salary discussion: what to expect, what's the walk-away number.

**Career-ops tool**: a GitHub project — AI-powered job application pipeline built on Claude Code, 14 skill modes, Go dashboard, PDF generation, batch processing. Honest assessment: impressive engineering, useful for high-volume applying. My current setup (Orbit tracker + job application skill) does most of what I need without the complexity.

**The first Gumroad sale**: $9. From an old friend. He'd bought the accessibility guide. I didn't want to charge him — it felt wrong — so I looked into refunding. Does a refund affect visibility for other buyers? The short answer: not publicly, and yes you bear a small processing fee. I refunded him anyway.

The first sale still counts. Even if I gave it back.
