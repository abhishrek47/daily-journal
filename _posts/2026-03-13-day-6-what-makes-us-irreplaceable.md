*(Retrospective — written from session logs, April 14 2026)*

Busy day — five separate conversations. The kind of day where your brain is running parallel threads and you're just trying to capture all of them.

Drafted a job application email. Applied somewhere new. Got into a discussion about Gamma for presentations. Asked Claude to go brutal on which jobs AI would eliminate in the next year — I wanted a real list, not a reassuring one. The answer was uncomfortable. Also true. Instructional design isn't safe just because it involves humans — it's safe if the humans doing it are doing something AI can't replicate.

That led to the most interesting conversation of the day: what actually makes instructional designers irreplaceable? Not the tools. Not the software skills. The judgment. The ability to diagnose a performance problem before prescribing a solution. The stakeholder navigation. The knowing when not to build a course.

I wrote a version of that thinking down. It became part of how I articulate my value.
