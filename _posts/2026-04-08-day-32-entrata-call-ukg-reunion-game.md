*(Retrospective — reconstructed April 14 2026)*

Three conversations. One of them genuinely significant.

**Entrata India — got the call**. A recruiter reached out. Senior L&D Partner, Pune. She's scheduling Round 1 this week. I built a full research report before responding: Entrata is a property management software company, Blackstone-backed, $4.3B valuation, growing India office. Mapped my skills against the JD carefully. The concern I had to sit with honestly: approximately 40% of the role leans on facilitation and L&D program management — things I moved away from post-COVID because I genuinely don't want to do them. The 60% that's instructional design, systems thinking, and technical development is where I live. The question is whether to pursue it anyway and see where it goes, or wait for a better-matched role. Decided to take the call. You don't skip conversations with companies that are growing and technically interesting.

**UKG Lead Talent Development Manager (Pune, hybrid)**: UKG is a solid US-headquartered HR/workforce company. The role leaned program management over technical design. The hybrid component also gave me pause. Screened it and got the picture.

**School reunion game**: 225 people from IBPES school, last together 20 years ago. The WhatsApp group came alive and someone suggested a virtual game. Built a multiplayer Pictionary — shareable session links, everyone draws and guesses, no screenshot-based nonsense. The multiplayer architecture was the challenge. Got it working.

The Entrata call is the one to watch this week.
